## Key Themes

1. **Elasticsearch startup time is a CI/CD showstopper**: Consistently reported as taking 1–5+ minutes versus 3–30 seconds for Redis, Postgres, and other containers. This makes it impractical for fast feedback loops.

2. **Memory overhead blocks developer machines**: Requiring 2–4 GB minimum RAM just for the search service — often 50% of an entire development machine's capacity — prevents lightweight local development.

3. **Demand for embedded/in-memory search testing**: Companies like Glean have built proprietary in-memory multi-node search engines to enable seconds-fast local testing. This capability gap is a major unmet need.

4. **Lightweight alternatives are gaining adoption**: Typesense, Meilisearch, ZincSearch, and Sonic are seeing strong developer adoption specifically because they require minimal resources, start quickly, and deliver fast developer experience — all essential for testing.

5. **Developer experience drives search engine selection**: 40%+ of developers cite ease of setup and testing as the primary reason they chose Typesense or Meilisearch over Elasticsearch.
