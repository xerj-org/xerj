# Vendor Business Practices and Support

## Severity: MODERATE | Frequency: LOW-MODERATE

---

## Core Complaints

### Sales Practices
- "Cowboy tactics during contract renewal -- take our terms or die" -- Andreas H., System Administrator, Retail (Capterra)
- "Constantly changing contact persons" and "invoices with changing account details"
- "Elastic isn't very good at selling the product in a way that helps internally" -- making it difficult to justify to leadership
- Enterprise sales model with "high vendor management overhead"
- 3-6 month acquisition timelines
- Made it "very difficult to try it out at scale" and "only wanted to talk to the CTO"

### Feature Offloading
- "From version 9, you will have to self-manage your Elastic web crawlers which shifts the responsibility on the customer to provide the infrastructure" -- G2 reviewer
- "There is also the ongoing support that comes with this too"
- Previously included features being moved to customer responsibility

### Support Quality
- "Documentation and support has been a little muddled" -- Paul S., CEO, Legal Services (Capterra)
- Enterprise-scale ELK deployments encounter "subtle but severe issues such as query latency spikes, dropped log events, or index corruption" that require specialized support
- Limited cloud trial of only 14 days, "forcing commercial commitments without extended evaluation"

### AI Feature Quality
- "The latest AI additions are useless, there needs to be some templating to make things simpler" -- G2 reviewer
- Company rebranded as "The Search AI company" but AI features considered immature

---

## XERJ.ai Response
- No aggressive sales tactics (self-hosted product)
- All features available in M1 -- no paywalled capabilities
- Transparent roadmap aligned with PROD.brief.md
- No feature offloading or version surprise removals
