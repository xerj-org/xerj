STORAGE
BECOMES AI.

XERJ has no illustrated logotype. The logo is the word — and two 1 px rules that carry the story of the product. A paper-coloured line under XERJ — the row in storage. A yellow line over .AI — the indexed value served back as intelligence. Each rule is the colour of the text it connects to. The offset between them is deliberate: storage is the input, AI is the output, the wordmark is the transformation in between.

LANGUAGE·WHY IT LOOKS LIKE THIS

GOLD.
LINE.
TYPE.

The brand is made of three decisions and nothing else: a single gold accent, a 1 px stroke, and typography doing the work a chart would do. Each has a reason. None is decoration.

THE GOLD IS A STANDARD, NOT A COLOUR.

Every industry that has a reference calls it the gold standard. A gold bar is 99.99 % pure by defined assay. A gold medal is the winner. A gold seal is notarised truth. The word holds across centuries — the Roman solidus, the Arabic dinar, gold leaf on Qur'anic manuscripts, gold trim on a thobe, gold on any document that has to still mean something ten years later. Gold earned the name because gold does not tarnish.

XERJ takes its name from the Arabic word for life — ḥayāh (حياة). In that tradition, gold carries weight beyond wealth. A gift of gold marks a milestone. Gold thread marks craft meant to outlast the wearer. Gold ink on parchment marks a truth the writer was willing to sign. Across all of it, gold is applied only to things already good enough to carry its weight. Success, not material.

We use the accent (#FFC400) the same way. It marks the one number a chart is about, the one word a sentence is pointing to, the p95 that says we are shipping. If a designer paints a row heading yellow because it "looks nice", they have devalued the stamp for the thing that actually deserves it.

1 PX IS THE MINIMUM THAT STILL CARRIES MEANING.

Any modern screen can render a 1 px line. Most brands do not trust that to be enough. They add a gradient to give it depth. They add a shadow to give it weight. They widen it to 2 px "for emphasis". Each addition is a concession that the idea underneath was not strong enough to carry the line alone.

Our rule is absolute. Every hairline, every axis tick, every divider, every border, every bar in every chart: one pixel. Never wider. Never gradient. Never feathered. If a design needs a thicker stroke to be legible, the design has a bigger problem that the thicker stroke is covering up. Fix the idea, not the border.

This is engineering discipline, not aesthetic minimalism. Engineers do not ship a thousand lines of code when ten will do. Designers who have lived inside production systems do not ship 3 px borders when 1 px is sufficient. The narrowest line that still carries the information is the correct line.

TYPE IS THE CLEANEST FORMAT AN AI CAN EXPOSE.

An AI system outputs text. A log line is text. A query plan is text. A vector is a sequence of numbers rendered as text. A retrieval result is a snippet of text ranked against other snippets of text. The entire observable surface of a language model — prompt, response, embedding, audit trail — is plain text.

Marketing designers usually translate that text into pictures: illustrations, flowcharts, isometric servers with tiny humans standing next to them. Every translation loses fidelity. A query result drawn as a generic "document" icon tells you nothing; the same result as monospaced JSON tells you everything.

We do the opposite. Our dashboards are tables with good typographic taste. Our charts are 1 px strokes over a typographic axis. Our marketing pages render the same numbers our customers see on their consoles, at the same precision, without stylisation. Type is the native format of an AI system. Any other format is a translation away from the truth. Typography is not a substitute for an infographic — it is the infographic.

00·MARK SYSTEM

STORED BELOW.
SERVED ABOVE.

The mark is a two-stroke diagram of the product. The paper rule connects to XERJ from below — the storage layer, the rows on disk, the segment on the WAL. The yellow rule connects to .AI from above — the answer the agent reads back, the retrieval, the ranked vector. Each line is the colour of the half it anchors. They meet at the . — the exact boundary between stored bytes and served intelligence.

a · WORDMARK · STORAGE ↦ AI
b · LETTER MARK · KEY VALUE
c · SYSTEM LINE · SPAN

Alignment is measured from the baseline (y=170) and cap-top (y=70), not from the EM box — so the gap between each rule and its text is exactly 30 px at every scale. No optical nudging, no "looks right to me". Geometry wins.

01·PRIMARY WORDMARK

XERJ.AI

The primary lockup. The root word is set in paper (#F4F2EC) on night (#0B0B0D). The suffix .AI takes the accent (#FFC400). This is the only place where accent is applied to a character inside the wordmark — the short form XERJ is never coloured.

01 · NIGHT · PRIMARY · DUAL-RULE WORDMARK

Rule of colour: a rule is always the colour of the text it touches. The paper line under XERJ is paper (#F4F2EC); the yellow line over .AI is yellow (#FFC400). Never cross-colour. Never extend one rule to touch the other half of the word. Each rule owns exactly its half of the record.

02·SHORT FORM & LETTER MARK

SHORT.
SHORTER.

When space is tight — app title bars, compact headers, system tray — the short form XERJ is used without the .AI suffix. At favicon scale only a single X remains. The letter mark is the only glyph the brand can be reduced to.

02a · SHORT FORM · PAPER RULES
02b · LETTER MARK · THE KEY

When the .AI drops off, the rule system simplifies: both rules take the colour of whatever's left. Short form = XERJ + two paper rules. Letter mark = X + two yellow rules. Single-colour, symmetric, same 30 px gap above and below.

03·SYSTEM LINE

SYSTEM LINE.
WHERE THE WORDMARK WON'T FIT.

The system line is the tiny, tracked-out, monospaced signature used on footers, dashboards, PDF page headers, and email signatures. It is set in IBM Plex Sans 600 at 13 px with 0.24 em tracking and a middle dot separator. Never substitute it for the wordmark on covers, heroes, or product splashes — it is deliberately quieter.

03 · SYSTEM LINE · DUAL RULES IN MINIATURE

Approved second-tag variants: OBSERVE (default) · INDEX (docs) · EXPLAIN (compliance surfaces) · QUERY (product dashboards). One tag per surface, never stacked.

04·COLOR TREATMENTS

ONE MARK.

The wordmark exists in three treatments — night (primary), day (courtesy), and mono (for places we don't control, like a customer's slide deck or a partner's press release). The mono treatment keeps the .AI suffix but drops the accent colour, because we don't ship neon yellow on someone else's background.

05·CLEAR SPACE

CLEAR SPACE
= HEIGHT OF X.

The minimum padding around the wordmark on every side is equal to the cap height of the letter X at whatever size the mark is rendered. This rule is absolute — no edge of the wordmark ever sits closer than one X to a container border, a photo crop, another logo, or an interface chrome element.

06·MINIMUM SIZE

THE LADDER.
WHAT FITS WHERE.

Every surface has a pre-approved size. Below the minimum the wordmark is replaced by the short form, then by the letter mark, then it is removed entirely. The mark is never rendered below 16 px cap height — below that it becomes unreadable and damages the brand.

160 PX · DUAL RULES HERO · LANDING ONLY
96 PX · DUAL RULES COVER · SLIDE TITLE
56 PX · DUAL RULES SECTION · DOC HEAD
32 PX · NO MARKS XERJ.AI NAV · APP HEADER
20 PX · NO MARKS XERJ.AI FOOTER · SIGNATURE

Both rules are drawn at a fixed 1 px weight regardless of wordmark size. Below 56 px the rules start to compete with the letterforms visually, so they drop off entirely — the wordmark stands on its typography alone from the nav bar down.

07·DO / DON'T

WHAT SHIPS.
WHAT DOESN'T.

DO Set the wordmark in Big Shoulders Display 900, all caps, +0.24 em tracking. Accent the .AI suffix only.
DO Place on a solid night (#0B0B0D) or solid day (#F6F4EE) surface. Nothing in between.
DO Keep one full X-height of clear space on every side — including above and below.
DO Drop to the short form at 16 px, then to the letter mark, then omit the logo entirely rather than shrink it further.
DO Colour each rule to match the text it touches — paper under XERJ, yellow over .AI. The rule is an extension of the glyph colour, not a separate ornament.
DO Draw rules at true 1 px stroke with stroke-linecap: square. Position them 30 px from baseline and 30 px from cap-top — measured, not eyeballed. Drop them below 56 px wordmark size.
DON'T Outline, stroke, emboss, or add a drop shadow. The mark is a solid typographic form. One weight, zero effects.
DON'T Tint the root word. XERJ is always paper-on-night or ink-on-day. Never yellow, never red, never blue.
DON'T Place the wordmark on photography, gradients, screenshots, or any surface with more than 5 % visual texture.
DON'T Rotate, skew, stretch, condense, or redraw the letterforms. If it is not Big Shoulders 900 at +0.24 em, it is not the wordmark.
DON'T Lock the wordmark up with an icon, mascot, illustration, or partner logo without a full X-height gap between them.
DON'T Extend one rule past the XERJ/.AI boundary to underline the whole wordmark — that collapses the two-colour story into a single underline. The offset between the rules is the mark.
DON'T Cross-colour the rules (yellow line under XERJ, paper line over .AI). The rule always matches the text it connects to, never the opposite half.
DON'T Put both rules on the same side of the wordmark. The rule over .AI must be above cap-top; the rule under XERJ must be below baseline. Never co-planar.

SHARP LINES.
SHARPER DATA.

The two rules that carry the wordmark — 1 px and typography — are not decoration specific to a logo page. They are the same rules the product uses to render data. An AI data company that cannot show a chart cleanly cannot sell storage cleanly either.
