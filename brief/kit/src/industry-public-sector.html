XERJ.AI02 · PUBLIC SECTOR AI PROBLEM
02·WHY THIS MATTERS NOW

AI MANDATES
MEET THE
ATO REALITY.

Every national government has published an AI strategy. Every agency has been told to adopt one. Every CIO has discovered the same constraint: the AI retrieval stack most commercial vendors ship cannot pass an ATO (Authority-to-Operate) review because it depends on infrastructure outside the perimeter.

DATA SOVEREIGNTY
Citizen data, classified material, and intelligence must not leave the national perimeter. A hosted vector DB in a commercial region fails before the conversation starts.
AIR-GAP REQUIREMENT
Classified networks, SCIFs, and defence enclaves have no internet. Installing, updating, and operating the retrieval layer must work offline with signed artifacts.
LONG ATO CYCLES
A new piece of infrastructure takes quarters to accredit. The less the retrieval stack is — binaries, network dependencies, third-party SaaS — the faster the ATO.
FedRAMP / IL5 / CJIS
Controls frameworks vary by mission. The retrieval layer must produce audit artifacts in the shape regulators already accept. Custom logging pipelines delay everything.
