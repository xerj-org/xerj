XERJ.AI02 · THE FINSERV AI PROBLEM
02·WHY THIS MATTERS NOW

AI TRANSFORMATION
MEETS THE
DATA RULE BOOK.

Every board agenda in banking and insurance names AI productivity as a top-three priority. Every regulator has responded with guidance — SR 11-7, EBA on model risk, PRA SS1/23, NAIC Bulletin. The AI platform conversation is no longer about what model to use; it is about whether the retrieval layer and the audit trail beneath it meet the rule book.

DATA SOVEREIGNTY
GDPR, DORA, cross-border data rules, national privacy acts. Your retrieval layer cannot call a vendor API in a different jurisdiction. Self-hosted is not a preference — it is a constraint.
MODEL-RISK GOVERNANCE
SR 11-7 and equivalents require documented inputs, outputs, and validation evidence for every AI decision. The retrieval layer is part of the model chain and must produce its own audit artifact per query.
FRAUD & AML LATENCY
Transaction monitoring and fraud decisions increasingly rely on vector similarity to historical patterns. The retrieval has to fit inside the payment-authorization budget — milliseconds, not seconds.
LEGACY STACK DRAG
Three separate retrieval stacks for security, compliance, and analyst productivity. Each has a dedicated team, contract, and audit. AI programs inherit the sum of their complexity.
